Switzerland

Location

Switzerland, federated country of central Europe. Switzerland’s administrative capital is Bern, while Lausanne serves as its judicial centre. Switzerland’s small size—its total area is about half that of Scotland—and its modest population give little indication of its international significance. A landlocked country of towering mountains, deep Alpine lakes, grassy valleys dotted with neat farms and small villages, and thriving cities that blend the old and the new, Switzerland is the nexus of the diverse physical and cultural geography of western Europe, renowned for both its natural beauty and its way of life. Aspects of both have become bywords for the country, whose very name conjures images of the glacier-carved Alps beloved of writers, artists, photographers, and outdoor sports enthusiasts from around the world.

General Knowledge

There are 7000 lakes in Switzerland. The water in these lakes is so clear that you can drink from rivers and lakes. If you can't see the bottom of a lake, that lake is considered polluted. 25% of its population is foreign. Switzerland has one of the highest rates of foreigners of any nation. More than 80% of foreigners living in Switzerland are from European countries and almost half of them come only from France, Germany, Italy, Portugal. It has 4 national languages. French (20.4%), German (64%), Italian (6.5%) and Romansh (just over 1%).
Unemployment rate is low. In 2017 statistics, Switzerland's unemployment rate was only around 4.8%. It is the country with the highest wages and job security. Switzerland is the third country with the highest wages and job security among the Organization for Economic Development and Cooperation (OECD) countries.
Gotthard tunnel in Switzerland is the longest tunnel in the world. Switzerland's Gotthard tunnel is the longest in the world at 57km (7km longer than the Channel Tunnel between England and France). It took 17 years to complete. The longest staircase in the world is found in Switzerland. This staircase is the longest in the world, with 11,674 steps and a height of 1,669 m (5,476 ft). The staircase is not open to the public, but a public run is held once a year to enjoy the view (and break a sweat). It is one of the most expensive places to live. Zurich is the third most expensive city in the world. The average house price is CHF 13,000 ($13,036) per square meter, while the average monthly rental price is: CHF 2,324 ($2,330) for a three-bedroom flat. However, the annual salary averages CHF 103,296 ($103k).

Foods

Cheese Fondue
When you think of Swiss food, the first thing that comes to mind is cheese fondue, arguably the most popular dish of Swiss cuisine. This dish, which is preferred during the winter months, is prepared by adding melted cheese, white wine, garlic, cornmeal and some maraschino liqueur, and is served in a ceramic pot with a small portable stove underneath. Pieces of bread are stuck in long fondue forks and dipped into the fondue and eaten.
Rösti
Rösti, Switzerland's most famous potato dish, is made by frying grated potatoes. Crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, this grater is added with bacon, onions, cheese and applesauce.
Zürcher Geschnetzeltes
This dish, which can be considered the signature of Canton Zürich, is made by adding white wine, mushrooms and cream sauce to cubed meat and is usually served with rösti.

Sprüngli, which was founded in 1845 as a small confectioner, later acquired Lindt and became one of the most important chocolate producers in the world. Confiserie Sprüngli has 4 different branches in Zurich. Especially delicious truffles in Sprüngli; Hazelnut, cocoa, white chocolate, milk, dark, coffee, champagne and many more chocolate flavors are waiting for you. Cities

Bern
Bern is one of the most important destinations in Switzerland is also the capital of Switzerland. A dreamy place to visit with lots of architecture and natural sights to check out. Bern was built around a swindler on the Aare River and has its origins in the 12th century.
Wandering the cobbled cobbled streets, you'll pass many old buildings. The medieval architecture of the city is called the Old Town, or Altstadt, which is protected by unesco world heritage status.
This city is an ideal place for people who enjoy seeing large art and sculpture collections. Bern's cultural scene includes several impressive museums and galleries, such as the Bern History Museum and the Zentrum Paul Kleeg. Bern is home to Switzerland's tallest cathedral, the bernen minister, and the medieval clock tower, the Zytglogge. Besides the collection of historical sites, the city also offers many fun things to do.

Zurich
Zurich is Switzerland's largest city and there are many great things to do and see during your stay. The city is home to some of the country's largest museums and art galleries. For this reason, it is referred to as the historical, cultural and financial center of the nation. Zurich is home to the Landesmuseum, also known as the Swiss National Museum. It contains art collections dating back to the prehistoric period.
Along with numerous museums and galleries, you can also visit Lake Zurich, which offers breathtaking views. There is no better way to explore the city than on a boat trip. If you need another reason to believe that Zurich is worth a visit, consider that Mercer ranks it as one of the cities with the best quality of life in the world.
