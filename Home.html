A Tradition Worth 40 Years of Remembrance: Turkish Coffee

Turkish coffee, which is easily found in every corner in Turkey, was added into UNESCO’s Intangible Cultural World Heritage list in 2013. Having a special place in this culture for many years, Turkish coffee is a tradition that lived through history and will continue to live on.There are many ways to produce or consume coffee. Each glass or cup of coffee tells another story. Cooked with a method different than traditional brewing techniques, Turkish coffee is a world-famous taste. Its origin is Yemen. In 1517, Yemen joined Ottoman territory. In 1543, the governor of Yemen at the time brought coffee to the Ottoman palace. The coffee was loved and frequently consumed.
